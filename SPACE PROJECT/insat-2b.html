INSAT-2B

Mission Overview:


INSAT-2B was an integral part of India's space program, contributing significantly to the country's communication and meteorological capabilities. Launched on July 23, 1993, it operated in geostationary orbit, providing continuous coverage over the Indian subcontinent. Equipped with a range of payloads, including telecommunications transponders and meteorological instruments, INSAT-2B played a crucial role in advancing various sectors. One of the primary functions of INSAT-2B was to enhance telecommunications services across India. With 12 C-band and 6 extended C-band transponders, it facilitated television broadcasting, telephony, and data transmission, particularly in remote and rural areas where terrestrial communication infrastructure was limited. This helped bridge the communication gap and improve connectivity nationwide, fostering socio-economic development. In addition to telecommunications, INSAT-2B carried meteorological payloads to monitor weather patterns and provide essential data for weather forecasting. Its real-time observations of atmospheric conditions, cloud cover, and precipitation were instrumental in disaster management, agriculture, aviation, and other sectors reliant on accurate weather information. INSAT-2B significantly bolstered India's meteorological capabilities, enhancing the country's ability to predict and respond to natural disasters. INSAT-2B had a successful operational lifespan, serving India's communication and meteorological needs for several years. However, like all satellites, it eventually reached the end of its operational life and was decommissioned. Its legacy, however, lives on, as it paved the way for subsequent generations of satellites with improved capabilities. INSAT-2B remains a testament to India's space prowess and its commitment to leveraging space technology for the betterment of its citizens and the region as a whole..
