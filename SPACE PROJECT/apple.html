APPLE

Mission Overview:


Apple's satellite communication capabilities have significantly advanced with the introduction of the Emergency SOS via satellite feature available on the iPhone 14 and 15 models. This feature allows users to send emergency messages when they are outside the range of cellular and Wi-Fi networks, enhancing safety during outdoor activities or in remote areas.

Key Features of Apple's Satellite Service Emergency SOS via Satellite: Activation: This feature is automatically prompted when an iPhone 14 or 15 user attempts to make an emergency call in an area without cellular or Wi-Fi coverage. Users are guided to point their phone towards a satellite for a connection. Functionality: Users answer a few questions on the screen to describe their emergency, which helps streamline the data sent. The information is then transmitted to a Globalstar satellite, relayed to a ground station, and forwarded to emergency services or Apple’s relay centers if local services cannot receive texts​ (Apple)​​ (SpaceNews)​. Globalstar Partnership: Apple has invested $450 million to enhance Globalstar's infrastructure, including upgrading ground stations and developing a new generation of satellites. Globalstar's network is predominantly dedicated to Apple's services, with 85% of its capacity reserved for Apple​ (Apple)​​ (SpaceNews)​.

Roadside Assistance: In partnership with AAA, Apple offers roadside assistance via satellite in the U.S. This service is free for two years for new iPhone 14 and 15 users.
AAA members can access this service on a pay-per-use basis​ (Apple)​. Find My App Integration: Users can share their location via satellite through the Find My app, ensuring they can update friends and family about their whereabouts even when off the grid​ (Apple)​​ (GearJunkie)​. Impact and Future Prospects Apple's satellite service has already proven life-saving in various situations, such as rescuing a man who fell off a cliff and locating lost hikers.
